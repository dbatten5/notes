\(\sigma\)-field

A collection \(\mathcal{F}\) of \(\Omega\) is a \(\sigma\)-field if the following are satisfied:

  • \(\empty \in \mathcal{F}\)
  • if \(A_1, A_2, \ldots, A_n \in \mathcal{F}\), then \(\bigcup_{i=1}^\infin A_i \in \mathcal{F}\)
  • if \(A \in \mathcal{F}\) then \(A^{c} \in \mathcal{F}\)

That is, the empty set is in \(\mathcal{F}\), itâ€™s closed under countable union (and intersection), and closed under taking the complement.

The smallest \(\sigma\)-field is \(\{\empty, \Omega\}\).

Another not so small \(\sigma\)-field is \(\{\empty, A, A^{c}, \Omega\}\), assuming \(A\) is a subset of \(\Omega\).
